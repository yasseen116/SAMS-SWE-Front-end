About the Department

Excellence in Software Engineering Education and Research

Department Overview

Who We Are

The Department of Software Engineering at the Faculty of Computers & Artificial Intelligence is committed to advancing software engineering education and research. Our department serves as a hub of innovation, bringing together world-class faculty, cutting-edge research facilities, and talented students.

Established to meet the growing demand for skilled software engineers in the industry, our department has grown to become a leader in software engineering education in the region. We offer comprehensive programs designed to prepare students for successful careers in software development, systems architecture, artificial intelligence, and cybersecurity.

Our commitment extends beyond the classroom, fostering industry partnerships, collaborative research, and a vibrant campus community that encourages innovation and intellectual growth.

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To educate and inspire the next generation of software engineers who will drive innovation and solve complex technological challenges. We are committed to:

  • Providing world-class education in software engineering principles and practices
  • Conducting cutting-edge research that advances the field
  • Fostering partnerships with industry and academic institutions
  • Developing ethical professionals who contribute to society

Our Vision

To be a globally recognized leader in software engineering education and research. We envision:

  • A center of excellence attracting top talent from around the world
  • Innovation labs driving technological advancement
  • Graduates who are leaders in their fields
  • Strong impact on industry and society through research and education

University Regulations

Guidelines and policies governing academic conduct and student responsibilities

Academic Standards

  • Minimum GPA of 2.0 required to remain in good academic standing
  • Students must complete courses within the prescribed time frame
  • Retaking of courses is permitted; the highest grade will be used for GPA calculation
  • Academic probation is imposed for GPA below 2.0; dismissal may follow persistent low performance
  • Withdrawal from courses must be done by the published deadline

Academic Integrity

  • Plagiarism, cheating, and academic dishonesty are strictly prohibited
  • All assignments must be original work unless otherwise specified
  • Proper citation of sources is required for all referenced material
  • Violations will result in grade penalties, course failure, or expulsion
  • Students must maintain academic integrity in all coursework and examinations

Attendance Requirements

  • Regular attendance is mandatory for all courses
  • Excessive absence may result in course failure
  • Instructors maintain attendance records and may exclude students with poor attendance
  • Excused absences require documented evidence (medical certificates, etc.)
  • Permission to miss classes must be obtained from the department chair

Registration & Enrollment

  • Students must register during the designated registration period each semester
  • Course prerequisites must be satisfied before enrollment
  • Add/drop deadlines must be observed as per the academic calendar
  • Full-time status requires enrollment in a minimum of 12 credit hours
  • Maximum credit hours per semester are specified by the degree program

Progression Requirements

  • Students must complete prerequisite courses before advancing to higher-level courses
  • Satisfactory progress toward degree completion is required
  • Students with excessive incomplete grades may be required to withdraw
  • Grade replacement policies allow substitution of grades under specific conditions
  • Junior and senior standing requires completion of specific credit hours

Conduct & Discipline

  • Students must adhere to the university code of conduct at all times
  • Misconduct may result in disciplinary action ranging from warnings to expulsion
  • All students are expected to be respectful and professional in their interactions
  • Violations of campus policies will be addressed by student conduct offices
  • Students have the right to appeal disciplinary decisions through proper channels

Financial Policies

  • Tuition and fees must be paid by the deadline each semester
  • Late payment fees apply if payment is made after the deadline
  • Financial aid eligibility requires maintaining satisfactory academic progress
  • Refund policies vary based on withdrawal timing and circumstances
  • Students are responsible for understanding payment plans and deadlines

Rights & Responsibilities

  • Students have the right to fair and unbiased treatment in all academic matters
  • Instructors must provide clear syllabi and grading criteria at the course start
  • Students are responsible for maintaining their academic records and progress
  • Classroom equipment and university facilities must be used responsibly
  • Students must report grade disputes within the prescribed timeframe
